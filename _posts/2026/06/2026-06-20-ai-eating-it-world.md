## Where Are We Now? The AI Tsunami

By 2026, over **75% of IT companies** have integrated AI copilots into daily workflows. Generative AI tools can now write functional code in Python, JavaScript, SQL, and even detect bugs automatically. But the news isn't all doom — productivity has skyrocketed. According to [McKinsey's research](https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/mckinsey-explainers/what-is-generative-ai){:target="_blank"}, generative AI could add $2.6 trillion to $4.4 trillion annually to the global economy.

> **KEY STAT**
>
> GitHub Copilot increased developer productivity by 55% (Microsoft research). IT tasks are getting faster — but the human layer becomes more strategic.

## Will IT professionals lose jobs? The truth.

The World Economic Forum's [Future of Jobs Report](https://www.weforum.org/publications/the-future-of-jobs-report-2025/){:target="_blank"} predicts that while AI may automate 25% of routine IT tasks, it will also create **+35% new roles** in AI governance, integration, and human-AI collaboration by 2030. So not a net loss — a massive shift. Think of it like the shift from assembly lines to automation: more creative, high-value work emerges.

> **Key takeaway:** IT pros who embrace AI will thrive. Those who ignore it might struggle. The future belongs to "AI-augmented engineers" and "citizen developers" using no-code AI tools.

## What if AI keeps improving exponentially?

Let's imagine 2030: AI systems write 90% of production code, self-deploy, and self-heal. In that scenario, the IT field transforms into **strategic architecture, business alignment, and creative problem solving**. But here's the twist: human creativity, empathy, and domain expertise become the premium currency. AI doesn't understand corporate politics, user emotions, or ethical nuance — that's where IT experts become invaluable.

### Enterprises will

- Reduce junior dev hiring by 40% (but increase senior AI-architect roles)
- Adopt "AI SWAT teams" for hyper-automation
- Invest heavily in AI risk management

### Learning paths change

- Bootcamps shift from "learn to code" to "learn to prompt & verify"
- Degrees in AI-human interaction emerge
- Certifications like "Certified AI-Augmented Engineer"

## A Day in IT: 2020 vs. 2026+

- **2020 developer:** Writes REST API from scratch, debugs for 3h, writes unit tests manually, fixes bugs reported by QA.

- **2026 developer:** Describes the API to AI, gets working code + tests, reviews & tweaks, uses AI to find edge cases. Focuses on architecture & performance — 2x output.

- **IT Support then:** Answer repetitive password reset tickets.

- **IT Support now:** AI bot handles level 1, IT pros manage complex integrations and automation workflows.

### The Verdict: Upskill, Don't Panic

The IT field isn't dying — it's going through a **golden age of productivity**. Salaries for AI-literate IT experts are rising, remote collaboration with AI agents is the new normal, and the demand for creative problem-solvers is higher than ever. The key: learn to delegate to AI, but never forget your human edge — **creativity, ethics, and big-picture thinking**.
